01 В· Foundations

What is Trigonometry?

The word comes from the Greek trigonon (triangle) and metron (measure). At its core, trigonometry studies the fixed relationship between the angles of a right triangle and the ratios of its sides.

Why does it matter?

Every right triangle with the same angle Оё вҖ” no matter how big or small вҖ” has the exact same ratio between its sides. That single fact means that if you know one angle and one side of a triangle, you can find every other angle and side, without ever needing to measure them directly. This is the idea that let ancient surveyors measure mountains they could never climb.

Where it's used

  • Measuring heights of towers & mountains
  • Surveying and mapmaking
  • Marine & air navigation
  • Construction & architecture
  • Astronomy вҖ” distances to stars
  • Engineering & robotics
