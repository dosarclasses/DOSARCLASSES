01 · Foundations

What is Banking?

A bank is an institution that accepts deposits from the public and lends money for various purposes. This chapter focuses on one particular kind of bank account — the Recurring Deposit (RD) Account — where a depositor saves a fixed sum every month for a fixed period and receives the total deposit plus interest at maturity.

Why does it matter?

An RD account builds the habit of regular saving and rewards it with interest — but because each monthly deposit sits in the bank for a different length of time, the interest calculation is more interesting than a simple one-time deposit. Understanding this formula is central to solving every problem in this chapter.

Where it's used

  • Monthly savings plans (RD accounts)
  • Lump-sum investments (Fixed Deposits)
  • Everyday transactions (Savings & Current accounts)
  • Loans taken against deposits
  • Comparing returns across savings schemes

12 · One Page

Quick Revision

Key Facts

Interest: I = P × n(n+1)/2 × r/1200

Maturity Value: MV = P×n + I

Total deposited: P × n

SB interest: I = (Sum of monthly balances) × r/1200

Rules to Remember

SB interest uses balance between the 10th and last day

Premature closure ⟹ penal (lower) rate

Doubling P doubles I; doubling n more-than-doubles I

All Definitions & Rules (memorize this)

Exam Tips

  • Always compute n(n+1)/2 first, then multiply by P and r, then divide by 1200 — doing the division last avoids messy fractions.
  • Maturity Value always equals the total deposited (P×n) plus the interest (I) — never forget to add I.
  • When a question gives the maturity value and asks for the rate or installment, subtract P×n first to isolate the interest.
  • For SB account questions, remember interest depends on the minimum balance between the 10th and last day of each month, not the closing balance.

Recurring Deposit

Interest: I = P·n(n+1)/2·r/1200

Equivalent form: I = P·n(n+1)·r/2400

Maturity Value: MV = P·n + I

Total deposited: P·n

Savings Bank Account

Interest: I = (Σ qualifying balances)·r/1200

Qualifying balance: min. balance between 10th & last day

Deposits after the 10th don't count for that month

Rules of Thumb

Doubling P (n, r fixed) → I exactly doubles

Doubling r (P, n fixed) → I exactly doubles

Doubling n (P, r fixed) → I more than doubles

Premature closure → penal (lower) rate applies
