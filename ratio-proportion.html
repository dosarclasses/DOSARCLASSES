01 · Foundations

What is a Ratio?

A ratio compares two quantities of the same kind by division. Written a:b (or a/b), it tells you how many times bigger — or what fraction — one quantity is of another. A proportion is simply a statement that two ratios are equal.

Why does it matter?

Ratios let you scale things up or down while keeping their relationships intact — a recipe for 4 people can feed 12 without ever changing its "flavour," because every ingredient scales by the same factor. Proportion is the tool that makes this scaling precise and provable.

Where it's used

  • Scaling recipes and ingredients
  • Map scales & architectural models
  • Mixing paint, cement & concrete
  • Aspect ratios of screens & photos
  • Gear and pulley ratios
  • Dividing profit, rent & inheritance

Proportion & Continued Proportion

05 · Identity

Componendo-Dividendo

If a/b = c/d, then (a+b)/(a−b) = (c+d)/(c−d). Scale the ratio below by any factor k — the value of (a+b)/(a−b) never changes.

Animated example

Base ratio a:b = 4:3. Drag the slider to scale both by k, and watch (a+b)/(a−b) stay fixed at 7.

a = 4k = 4 b = 3k = 3
Check (4+3)/(4−3) = 7/1 = 7 — always true, no matter what k is.

Quick Revision

Key Facts

Duplicate ratio: a²:b² · Triplicate: a³:b³

Sub-duplicate: √a:√b · Sub-triplicate: ∛a:∛b

Proportion: ad = bc

Continued proportion: b² = ac

Property Facts

Invertendo: b/a = d/c

Alternendo: a/c = b/d

Componendo-Dividendo: (a+b)/(a−b) = (c+d)/(c−d)

All Definitions & Properties (memorize this)

Exam Tips

  • Always check that a ratio compares quantities of the same kind before simplifying it.
  • When a proportion involves surds, look for a chance to apply componendo-dividendo before squaring anything.
  • To combine two ratios like a:b and b:c, make the common term (b) equal in both before joining them.
  • Remember: mean proportional is a geometric mean (√ac), never an arithmetic average.
