01 · Foundations

What is Statistics?

Statistics is the branch of mathematics dealing with collecting, organizing and summarizing data. This chapter focuses on measures of central tendency — the mean, median and mode — for both ungrouped and grouped data, along with cumulative frequency and the ogive.

Why does it matter?

Raw data — marks of a class, daily temperatures, sales figures — is hard to interpret on its own. A single representative number, called a measure of central tendency, summarizes the whole data set at a glance. Different measures (mean, median, mode) each capture "the centre" in a different way, and knowing which one to use — and how to compute it from a grouped table — is the core skill of this chapter.

Where it's used

  • Summarizing exam marks or survey results
  • Comparing performance across groups
  • Reading cumulative frequency (ogive) graphs
  • Market research and quality control
  • Government census and population data
  • Weather and sports statistics reporting

05 · Identity

The Empirical Relationship

For a moderately skewed distribution, the mean, median and mode are connected by Mode = 3Median − 2Mean. Slide the mean below (with the median fixed at 30) and watch the mode recompute automatically from the formula.

Animated example

A distribution has median fixed at 30. Drag the slider to choose different values for the mean, and watch the mode update instantly using Mode = 3Median − 2Mean. This relationship is a well-tested approximation for moderately skewed data, not an exact identity — but ICSE questions generally expect you to apply it directly.

Mean = 28 Mode = 34
Check Median is fixed at 30, so Mode = 3(30) − 2(28) = 90 − 56 = 34 — sliding the mean always recomputes the mode from the same formula.

12 · One Page

Quick Revision

Key Facts

Mean (Direct): x̄ = Σfx/Σf

Mean (Step-Deviation): x̄ = A + (Σfu/Σf)×h

Median (Grouped): l + [(N/2−cf)/f]×h

Rule Facts

Mode (Grouped): l + [(f1−f0)/(2f1−f0−f2)]×h

Empirical: Mode = 3Median − 2Mean

Quartiles: Q1 at N/4, Q3 at 3N/4

All Definitions & Rules (memorize this)

Exam Tips

  • Always use the class mark (mid-value), never the class interval itself, when substituting x into the mean formula.
  • Locate the median class using cumulative frequency and N/2, not the ordinary frequency alone.
  • The modal class is the one with the highest ordinary frequency, not the highest cumulative frequency.
  • Remember to multiply back by h at the end of the step-deviation method — forgetting this is the single most common mistake.
