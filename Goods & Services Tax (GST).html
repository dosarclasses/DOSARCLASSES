01 · Foundations

What is GST?

Goods and Services Tax (GST) is a single indirect tax charged on the supply of goods and services in India, replacing older taxes like VAT, excise duty and service tax. It is charged on the taxable value of a sale and is always shown separately on the invoice.

Why does it matter?

GST is a destination-based tax — it is meant to reach the government of the state where the goods or services are finally consumed, not the state where they were produced. Splitting it into CGST, SGST and IGST is how the tax is shared correctly between the Central Government and the State Governments.

Where it's used

  • Retail bills for goods and services
  • Manufacturer → wholesaler → retailer chains
  • Restaurant and hotel invoices
  • E-commerce purchase receipts
  • Inter-state and intra-state trade
  • Input tax credit claims by dealers

05 · Identity

Input Tax Credit & Net GST Payable

A dealer only pays the government the difference between the GST he collects (output) and the GST he already paid on his purchases (input, or Input Tax Credit). Scale the taxable value below by any factor k — the ratio of GST to taxable value never changes, because the rate is fixed.

Animated example

A dealer buys goods at a taxable value of ₹4,000 (input) and sells them at a taxable value of ₹7,000 (output), both taxed at 18%. Drag the slider to scale both taxable values by k, and watch the net GST payable scale exactly the same way.

Input value = 4000k = 4000 Output value = 7000k = 7000
Check Net GST payable = 18% of (7000−4000) = ₹540 — always 18% of the value-added, no matter what k is.

12 · One Page

Quick Revision

Key Facts

Taxable Value = Marked Price − Discount

GST Amount = Taxable Value × Rate/100

Intra-state: CGST = SGST = Rate/2

Inter-state: IGST = full Rate

Rule Facts

Invoice Value = Taxable Value + GST Amount

Taxable Value (from bill) = Bill ÷ (1 + Rate/100)

Net GST Payable = Output GST − Input Tax Credit

All Definitions & Rules (memorize this)

Exam Tips

  • Always deduct the discount from the marked price first — GST is charged on the taxable value, never on the marked price.
  • Check whether the transaction is intra-state (CGST + SGST) or inter-state (IGST only) before splitting the tax.
  • To recover the taxable value from a GST-inclusive bill, divide by (1 + rate/100); never multiply.
  • Net GST payable by a dealer is always Output GST minus Input Tax Credit, not the output GST alone.

Input Tax Credit

Output GST = GST collected on sales

Input Tax Credit (ITC) = GST paid on purchases

Net GST Payable = Output GST − ITC
