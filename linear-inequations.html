01 · Foundations

What is a Linear Inequation?

While an equation says two expressions are equal, an inequation says they are not equal in a specific direction — one is less than, greater than, or at most/least the other. A linear inequation in one variable has the form ax + b < c (or >, ≤, ≥), where the variable appears only to the first power.

Why does it matter?

Most real situations involve limits, not exact values — a lift that can carry "at most 8 people," a budget of "no more than ₹2000," or an age that must be "at least 18." Inequations are the tool that turns these limits into solvable mathematics, giving not one answer but an entire range of valid answers.

Where it's used

  • Budgeting & spending limits
  • Speed limits & safety margins
  • Passing marks & grade cut-offs
  • Load and capacity limits
  • Age & eligibility criteria
  • Manufacturing tolerances

12 · One Page

Quick Revision

Key Facts

Open circle: < or > (boundary excluded)

Filled circle: ≤ or ≥ (boundary included)

Replacement set: values the variable may take (N, W, Z, R)

Solution set: subset of replacement set satisfying the inequation

Rule Facts

Add/Subtract same number: direction unchanged

Multiply/Divide by positive: direction unchanged

Multiply/Divide by negative: direction reverses

All Definitions & Rules (memorize this)

Exam Tips

  • Always check the replacement set before writing the final solution set — a "correct" x range still needs filtering to N, W, Z, or R.
  • Whenever you multiply or divide by a variable expression (not a plain number), first confirm its sign — you cannot flip blindly.
  • For combined inequations like −3 < 2x + 1 ≤ 7, perform every operation on all three parts at once, not just one side.
  • Draw the number line even when not asked — it catches sign-flip mistakes instantly.

ICSE Class 10 Mathematics

Symbols & Circles

< strictly less than — open circle

> strictly greater than — open circle

≤ less than or equal to — filled circle

≥ greater than or equal to — filled circle

≠ not equal to — open circle, both directions

Definitions

Linear inequation: ax + b ⋛ c

Replacement set: allowed values of x

Solution set: values of x satisfying the inequation

Combined inequation: a < x ≤ b

Interval notation: −1 < x ≤ 3 ⟹ (−1, 3]

Rules

Addition: a<b ⟹ a+c < b+c

Subtraction: a<b ⟹ a−c < b−c

Mult./Div. by c>0: a<b ⟹ ac < bc

Mult./Div. by c<0: a<b ⟹ ac > bc (flips!)

Transitive: a<b, b<c ⟹ a<c
