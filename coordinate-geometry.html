01 · Foundations

What is Coordinate Geometry?

Coordinate geometry gives every point on a plane a unique "address" — an ordered pair (x, y) — so that distances, midpoints, slopes and equations of lines can all be found using algebra instead of a ruler and protractor.

Why does it matter?

Once a shape is placed on the Cartesian plane, every geometric fact about it — length, midpoint, direction, area — becomes a calculation. This is the bridge between algebra and geometry: it lets us prove that points are collinear, that a triangle is right-angled, or that a quadrilateral is a parallelogram, all without drawing a single line.

Where it's used

  • GPS navigation and mapping
  • Computer graphics and game design
  • Proving properties of triangles and quadrilaterals
  • Finding the equation of a straight line
  • Testing collinearity of points
  • Engineering and architectural design

05 · Identity

Area of a Triangle

The area of a triangle depends only on its base and perpendicular height — not on how far the opposite vertex slides along a line parallel to the base. Slide vertex C below and watch the area stay exactly the same.

Animated example

Triangle ABC has A(1,1), B(5,1) fixed — a base of 4 units along the line y = 1 — and C(x,5), which slides horizontally along the line y = 5, four units above the base. Drag the slider to move C and watch the area formula stay locked at 8 sq units.

x-coordinate of C = 3 Area of △ABC = 8 sq units
Check Base AB = 4, height = 4, Area = ½ × 4 × 4 = 8 sq units — sliding C sideways never changes the area, because the base and height never change.

12 · One Page

Quick Revision

Key Facts

Distance: d = √[(x₂−x₁)²+(y₂−y₁)²]

Midpoint: ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2)

Slope: m = (y₂−y₁)/(x₂−x₁)

Line: y = mx + c

Rule Facts

Parallel: m₁ = m₂

Perpendicular: m₁ × m₂ = −1

Collinear: area of triangle = 0

All Definitions & Rules (memorize this)

Exam Tips

  • Keep the order of subtraction consistent — always (second point − first point) in both the numerator and denominator of the slope formula.
  • For section formula questions, be careful which point corresponds to m and which to n; a swapped ratio gives the wrong point entirely.
  • To test collinearity, compare slopes of two overlapping pairs of points, or check that the area-of-triangle formula gives zero.
  • Always simplify a fraction slope fully, and never write a vertical line's slope as 0 — it is undefined.

Coordinate Geometry — Formula Sheet

ICSE Class 10 Mathematics

Distance & Section

d = √[(x₂−x₁)² + (y₂−y₁)²]

Distance from origin = √(x²+y²)

Section: ((mx₂+nx₁)/(m+n), (my₂+ny₁)/(m+n))

Midpoint: ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2)

Slope & Equation of a Line

m = (y₂−y₁)/(x₂−x₁)

Slope-intercept form: y = mx + c

Point-slope form: y − y₁ = m(x − x₁)

Horizontal line slope = 0, vertical line slope = undefined

Parallel, Perpendicular & Area

Parallel: m₁ = m₂

Perpendicular: m₁ × m₂ = −1

Area = ½|x₁(y₂−y₃)+x₂(y₃−y₁)+x₃(y₁−y₂)|

Collinear ⟺ area = 0
